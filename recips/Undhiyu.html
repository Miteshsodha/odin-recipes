Undhiyu

ðŸ““Description:

A hearty and flavorful Gujarati curry made with a mix of vegetables, such as eggplant, potatoes, and surti papdi, cooked in a rich and spicy gravy.

ðŸ““Ingredients:

  1. For the Undhiyu:
    • 1 large eggplant, cut into 1-inch pieces
    • 2 large potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
    • 1 cup surti papdi (flat beans)
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as carrots, green beans, and peas)
    • 1/2 cup peanuts
    • 1/4 cup coconut
    • 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
    • Salt, to taste
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
    • 2 tablespoons jaggery powder
    • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  2. For the spice blend:
    • 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
    • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
    • 1 tablespoon cinnamon sticks
    • 1 tablespoon cardamom pods
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  3. Optional ingredients:
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (for garnish)
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh coconut (for garnish)

ðŸ““Steps:

  1. Prepare the Spice Blend
    • Roast the coriander seeds, cumin seeds, cinnamon sticks, and cardamom pods in a pan until fragrant.
    • Grind the roasted spices into a fine powder.
  2. Cook the Vegetables
    • Heat oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
    • Add the eggplant, potatoes, surti papdi, and mixed vegetables. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the Spice Blend and Gravy
    • Add the ground spice blend, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, salt, peanuts, and coconut to the saucepan. Stir well.
    • Add tamarind paste and jaggery powder. Stir well.
  4. Simmer the Undhiyu
    • Reduce heat to low and simmer the Undhiyu for 15-20 minutes or until the gravy thickens.
  5. Garnish and Serve
    • Garnish with chopped cilantro and coconut (if using).
    • Serve hot with roti or rice.
