A flavorful and aromatic one-pot dish made with saffron-infused basmati rice, mixed vegetables (such as peas, carrots, and corn), and a blend of warming spices (including cumin, coriander, and cinnamon). This easy-to-make and nutritious Pulav recipe is perfect for a quick lunch or dinner.

ðŸ““Ingredients:

  1. For the Pulav:
    • 1 cup basmati rice
    • 2 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon ghee or vegetable oil
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 small carrot, peeled and grated
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as peas, corn, and cauliflower)
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
    • Salt, to taste
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads, soaked in 1 tablespoon hot water
    • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  2. For the spice blend:
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 2-3 green cardamom pods
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  3. Optional ingredients:
    • 1/2 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cucumber (for garnish)
    • Lemon wedges (for serving)

ðŸ““Steps:

  1. Prepare the Rice
    • Rinse the basmati rice in a fine mesh sieve until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice in 2 cups of water for 30 minutes.
    • Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  2. Heat Oil and Add Spices
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
    • Add the cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder (if using). Let the spices sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Saute Onions and Garlic
    • Add the chopped onion and sautÃ© until translucent (3-4 minutes).
    • Add the minced garlic and sautÃ© for another minute.
  4. Add Mixed Vegetables
    • Add the grated carrot and mixed vegetables. SautÃ© for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add Rice and Spices
    • Add the soaked and drained rice to the saucepan. Stir to coat the rice with the spice mixture.
    • Add the cinnamon stick, green cardamom pods, salt, and garam masala powder. Stir well.
  6. Add Water and Saffron
    • Add 2 cups of water to the saucepan. Stir to combine.
    • Add the soaked saffron threads and their liquid. Stir gently.
  7. Cook the Pulav
    • Bring the mixture to a boil.
    • Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.
  8. Garnish and Serve
    • Garnish with chopped cilantro, mint leaves, and cucumber (if using).
    • Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
